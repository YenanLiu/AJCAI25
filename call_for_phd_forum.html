Call For Submissions: AJCAI25 PhD Forum

We warmly invite PhD students working in the field of Artificial Intelligence to participate in the PhD Forum at the upcoming Australasian Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence (AJCAI 2025). The PhD Forum is a key component of the conference and will be held in person in Canberra, Australia, in ** November 2025, running alongside the main conference program. The Forum provides an excellent platform for PhD students to present their research through poster sessions and to actively engage with senior researchers from both academia and industry. Participants will have the unique opportunity to discuss their research interests, exchange ideas, and receive constructive feedback from experienced scientists. The Forum also offers a supportive environment to ask career-related questions, gain insights into various career paths, and receive mentorship to help shape their professional development. Along the way, students will also build connections with peers and explore opportunities for collaboration and long-term friendships.

What to Expect

  • Poster Presentation: Showcase your research through a dedicated poster session.
  • Feedback and Mentoring: Receive constructive feedback and guidance from experienced researchers, industry professionals, and peers to help refine your work and shape your academic or career path.
  • Networking Opportunities: Engage with fellow PhD students, senior academics, and industry experts, and explore opportunities for collaboration and mentorship.
  • Best Poster Award: All posters presented at the PhD Forum will be considered for the AJCAI25 Best Poster Award, to be announced during the Forum.
  • Support: Please note that there is no additional financial support specifically for attending the PhD Forum.

Eligibility

  • Open to all current Honours, Master's, and PhD students.

Important Dates

  • Submission Deadline: 11:59 PM (AEST) mm dd, 2025
  • Notification of Acceptance: mm dd 2025
  • AJCAI25 PhD Forum Date: Nov dd 2025

Registration and Submission:

All submissions and registrations must be made via the Google Form:

  • For mentors, we expect you to have:
    • Extensive Research Experience: You should be a postdoctoral researcher or a professional working in academia or industry after obtaining a PhD degree.
  • For mentees, we expect you to:
    • Submit a Poster: Present your current, completed, or future research topics.
    • Provide Basic Information: Include your PhD stage, CV, research topic, career interests (academia or industry), and the questions you would like to discuss with a mentor.
